## short articles
Four broad fields of study are Humanities (arts, history, languages), Social Sciences (psychology, sociology, economics), Natural Sciences (biology, chemistry, physics, Earth science), and Formal Sciences (math, logic, computer science), often alongside Applied Sciences (engineering, medicine, business) that use knowledge from these areas for practical purposes. These categories encompass the vast spectrum of academic disciplines, from understanding human culture to exploring the fundamental laws of the universe and developing new technologies. 

Here are four distinct examples:

Humanities: Focuses on human culture, expression, and society, including fields like Literature, Philosophy, and History. 

Social Sciences: Studies human society and relationships, with examples such as Psychology, Sociology, and Political Science. 

Natural Sciences: Explores the physical and natural world, encompassing disciplines like Biology, Chemistry, and Physics. 

Applied Sciences: Applies scientific knowledge to practical problems, including Engineering, Health Sciences, and Business. 

Other Common Groupings

STEM: Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. 

Liberal Arts: A broad category combining humanities, social sciences, and sometimes natural sciences, focusing on broad knowledge and critical thinking.
