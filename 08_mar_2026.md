## vocabulary


## short article
"O'clock" is a contraction of the phrase "of the clock," used exclusively to indicate a specific hour (e.g., 4:00, "four o'clock") on a mechanical timepiece, distinguishing it from natural time like sundials. "Clock" is the physical device used to measure time. 

Key Differences and Usage:
O'clock: Used only with exact hours (1 through 12).
Example: "Let's meet at 5 o'clock."
It is not used with minutes (e.g., do not say "5:30 o'clock").
The 'o' is a shortening of "of the".
Clock: The noun representing the instrument.
Example: "Look at the clock."
It can be used to describe an position relative to a, as in "twelve o'clock". 

Origin:
The term originated around the 15th century as "of the clock" to distinguish mechanical, uniform hours from variable sun time. Over time, it was shortened to "o'clock".
