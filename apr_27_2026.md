## short article
Former Google CEO Eric Schmidt has warned that the United States is ill-equipped to handle the infrastructure demands and "natural limits" of the artificial intelligence boom, according to a July 2025 report from Fortune. 

While AI is considered tech's "holy grail," Schmidt highlighted that the industry's rapid growth faces significant hurdles: 

The Energy Crisis: Schmidt stated that AI's true limit is electricity, not chips, noting that the U.S. needs approximately 92 more gigawatts of power—roughly equivalent to 92 nuclear power stations—to support the AI revolution.

Infrastructure Shortages: He noted that essentially none of the necessary power facilities are currently being built, with only two constructed in the last 30 years, per Fortune.

Labor Deficit: The U.S. faces a deficit of nearly 400,000 workers in skilled trades, making it difficult to build the necessary data centers.

AI Self-Improvement: Schmidt has also warned that when AI starts to self-improve, society will need to "seriously think about unplugging it". 

Related Concerns from Tech Leaders:

Mo Gawdat (Former Chief Business Officer, Google X): Warned that AI will trigger a "white-collar bloodbath" and that the idea it will create jobs is "100% crap," according to Fortune.

Geoffrey Hinton (Former Google AI Researcher): Warned that only the "very skilled" will remain safe from AI-driven job displacement, says Fortune. 

Schmidt serves as chair of the Special Competitive Studies Project, a think tank focused on AI competitiveness.
