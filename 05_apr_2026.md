### colonialism
Colonialism is the practice of extending and maintaining political, social, economic, and cultural domination over a territory and its people by another people in pursuit of interests defined in an often distant metropole, who also claim superiority.

the policy or practice of acquiring full or partial political control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically.

### canon
A canon is an accepted rule, principle, standard, or official list, frequently used in religious, literary, or artistic contexts to define authorized works (e.g., the biblical canon or a fictional universe's canon). It signifies an established, authoritative, or authentic body of work, derived from the Greek kanon for a measuring rod. 

Usage Examples
Fictional/Media: "Is the new Star Wars spin-off actually canon, or is it just fan fiction?"
Literature: "Shakespeare’s plays are firmly established in the canon of English literature".
Religion: "The early church council established the canon of Scripture".
General Principle: "We must act according to the canons of good taste". 

Synonyms of Canon
Principle / Rule / Tenet: Basic, accepted rules.
Standard / Criterion / Benchmark: A measure of quality or authenticity.
Authorized/Accepted Works: The official collection.
Dogma / Credo: A settled, authoritative doctrine. 

Key Contexts
Religious: Rules or laws set by a church council (canon law).
Academic/Artistic: A collection of works considered most important.
Music: A melody repeated by other voices after a short delay.
